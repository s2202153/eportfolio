Project overview

This project is a collaborative effort to innovate a food printing system that enhances food labelling.

By the end of this project, the prototype should be able to extrude a layer of edible wax on food surfaces, while ensuring that the labelling created on the food surface remains readable/scannable after extrusion.

Project Details

The system consists of a 2D Food Printer and a Physical rig shown below.

    Practical Side:

  • Physical Rig is made with T-slots, 3D printed parts, acrylic and metal parts.
  • System can be split into 3 parts: Movement Rig, Extruder Rig and Control System.
  • All 3D printed parts are designed and assembled using SolidWorks and Fusion 360.(as shown below)
  • Movement Rig makes use of rotary movements from the motor to move horizontally along the guiding rods.
  • The Extruder makes use of the rotary movements from motor to control the deposition of edible wax.
  • All electrical components are controlled through Arduino Mega using C++.

    • Theory Side:

  • Electrical Components are chosen based on functionality, cost and calculations.
  • Budget was split into usable and reserved for any emergencies.
  • Risks were assessed to identify possible mitigation strategies.

Results & Future Work

    Results:

  • Movement Rig was able to move as desired.
  • Extruder was unable to deposit edible wax like intended due to low viscosity of wax.
  • However, it can be proven that edible wax can be used as a secondary layer to enhace food labels.

    • Future Work:

  • Use another material to seal the syringe to modified tip to fix leakage.
  • Calibrate extruder mechanism to control amount of wax deposited.
  • Add a temperature sensor to control heating to prevent burning of edible wax.
